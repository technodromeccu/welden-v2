WELDEN LEAD MANAGEMENT WORKFLOW

OVERVIEW

This document explains the current Welden lead workflow for admin and staff.

The system now works as a lead-only process. Every inquiry becomes a lead, is assigned to staff, followed up, and then moved to won or lost.

This guide covers:

- how leads enter the system
- the difference between a normal lead and a lead with quote
- the first-call deadline
- email notifications
- staff and admin responsibilities
- how follow-up should happen
- how leads are closed

WORKFLOW SUMMARY

Every lead should move through this process:

1. Lead enters the system
2. Lead is assigned to staff
3. Admin and assigned staff receive internal email notification
4. Staff reviews the lead
5. Staff makes the first call within 2 working days
6. Staff records the outcome and next step
7. Staff continues follow-up until the lead is won or lost

LEAD ENTRY

Leads can enter the system through:

- chatbot inquiry
- website consultation form

When a lead is created, the system stores:

- customer name
- email
- phone number
- company, if available
- requirement summary
- machine interest, if identified

LEAD TYPES

Normal Lead

A normal lead is a lead without a preliminary quotation.

Typical examples:

- machine suitability inquiry
- callback request
- technical question
- custom requirement

Main purpose of follow-up:

- understand the need
- qualify the opportunity
- decide the next commercial step

Lead With Quote

A lead with quote is a lead where the chatbot has already issued a preliminary quotation.

These leads should be treated with higher urgency because the pricing discussion has already started.

Main purpose of follow-up:

- confirm the customer received the quotation
- answer technical or commercial questions
- move the customer toward proposal, decision, won, or lost

FIRST CALL DEADLINE

Every new lead must receive a first staff call within 2 working days.

This is the first-response rule the team should follow.

The admin shows the first-call status clearly as:

- Call pending
- Call due today
- Call overdue
- First call complete

This helps admin and staff quickly identify which leads need action.

EMAIL NOTIFICATIONS

When a new lead is created, an internal email is sent through Resend to:

- the assigned staff member
- the internal admin notification email list

The email includes the key lead details and the first-call deadline.

LEAD STAGES

The active stages used by the team are:

- New
- Quoted
- Contact Scheduled
- Contacted
- Qualified
- Proposal Sent
- Won
- Lost

TEAM RESPONSIBILITIES

Admin Responsibilities

Admin should:

- monitor all new leads
- check that every lead has an owner
- check that the first call happens on time
- monitor quoted leads closely
- review overdue and delayed leads
- review won and lost closure quality

Staff Responsibilities

Staff should:

- review assigned leads quickly
- make the first call within 2 working days
- record notes clearly
- update the stage correctly
- set the next follow-up date when needed
- continue follow-up until the lead is closed

DAILY WORKING METHOD

For every active lead, the team should always know:

- who owns the lead
- whether it is a normal lead or a lead with quote
- whether the first call is pending, due, overdue, or complete
- what happens next
- when the next follow-up is due

If these points are clear, the lead is under control.

FOLLOW-UP PROCESS

Step 1: Review the lead

When a lead enters the system, staff should review:

- customer details
- requirement summary
- machine interest
- whether a quotation already exists
- whether immediate contact is needed

Step 2: Make the first call

The first staff call should happen within 2 working days.

The purpose of the first call is to:

- confirm the customer requirement
- understand seriousness and timing
- answer immediate questions
- decide the next commercial step

Step 3: Record the outcome

After the first call, staff should record:

- what the customer said
- whether the lead is serious
- whether the lead needs qualification, proposal, or close
- what the next action is
- when the next follow-up will happen

Step 4: Continue follow-up

Every active lead should have a clear next step.

A lead should not remain open without:

- an owner
- a current stage
- notes
- a next follow-up plan when the conversation is still active

SCENARIO HANDLING

Scenario 1: Normal lead

1. staff reviews the inquiry
2. staff makes the first call
3. staff qualifies the requirement
4. staff updates the stage
5. staff schedules the next follow-up if needed

Typical movement:

- New to Contacted
- Contacted to Qualified
- Qualified to Proposal Sent, Won, or Lost

Scenario 2: Lead with quote from chatbot

1. the lead enters as a lead with quote
2. staff reviews the quotation reference and customer requirement
3. staff calls the customer within the first-call deadline
4. staff confirms quotation receipt, interest, objections, and next decision step
5. staff continues follow-up until closed

Typical movement:

- Quoted to Proposal Sent
- Quoted to Won
- Quoted to Lost

Scenario 3: Customer does not answer

- staff logs the contact attempt
- staff schedules the next follow-up
- staff tries again at the next planned time

Scenario 4: Customer asks for callback later

- staff records the preferred callback time
- the lead is kept visible for scheduled follow-up
- staff calls again at the agreed time

FOLLOW-UP DISCIPLINE

The team should remember:

- internal updates are not the same as customer communication
- changing stage or adding notes does not mean contact happened
- a lead should only be treated as contacted when staff has actually made contact or logged the attempt properly

WORKING DAYS AND REMINDER WINDOW

Current working setup:

- working days: Monday to Friday
- working hours: 9:00 to 18:00
- reminder window: 4 hours before the follow-up target

HOW LEADS ARE CLOSED

Won

A lead is closed as won when:

- the commercial process is complete
- the customer confirms the deal
- the opportunity has successfully converted

Lost

A lead is closed as lost when:

- the customer declines
- the project does not move forward
- there is no active commercial opportunity left

FINAL OPERATING RULE

Every open lead should always have clear answers to these questions:

- Who owns it?
- What type of lead is it?
- Has the first call been completed?
- What is the next step?
- When is the next follow-up due?

If these answers are clear, the workflow is healthy and easy for both admin and staff to manage.
